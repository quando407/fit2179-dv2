A Crisis Three Decades in the Making

Since the EPBC Act came into force in 1992, the number of species listed as threatened has tripled. The rate of listing has accelerated dramatically after major ecological disasters.

Section 5

A History of Loss

Australia has the world's worst mammal extinction record. Since European settlement in 1788, more than 100 species have been lost forever.

⚠️

The Bramble Cay Melomys — A World First

In 2016, the Bramble Cay Melomys became the first mammal in the world to be declared extinct due to climate change. Rising sea levels inundated its only habitat — a tiny coral cay in the Torres Strait — destroying its food supply and shelter.
